What is an IRB?

Incident Review Board

An IRB is a structured, fact-focused meeting that occurs within 24 hours of any safety incident at Texas Pride Fuels. It brings together the Collaborative Leadership Team and the individuals involved to answer one question: why did this happen, and how do we prevent it from ever happening again?

Root Cause Analysis (RCA)

Rather than assigning blame, the IRB digs into the chain of events using the "5 Whys" method — asking why repeatedly until the true underlying cause is identified. Every RCA produces specific corrective actions with an assigned owner and a deadline.

Accountability without blame

IRBs are grounded in professionalism and mutual respect. The focus is on facts and data, not emotions. This creates a culture where employees feel safe surfacing issues early — before they become serious incidents.

Non-negotiables
★Begins with a Champions of Safety moment
★Must occur within 24 hours of the incident — no exceptions
★Focus on facts and data, not emotions or blame
★Every corrective action has an owner and a completion deadline
★Follow the approved IRB template for consistency
★Professionalism and mutual respect are mandatory
★Eliminate distractions — give your full attention
★Ask WHY multiple times to reach the true root cause
